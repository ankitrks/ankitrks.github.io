ROLAND GARROS 2013

CERMAK/HRADECKA WIN MIXED DOUBLES CROWN

Paris, France
by ATP Staff
06.06.2013

Czech duo Frantisek Cermak and Lucie Hradecka won the Roland Garros mixed doubles trophy on Thursday as they defeated Canada’sDaniel Nestor and Kristina Mladenovic of France 1-6, 6-4, 10-6 in the final in Paris.
"I feel great. It's a great success for me," said Cermak. "I'm very happy for it. I have to thank Lucie, because without her I wouldn't make it. It's a great feeling."

After a slow start, Cermak and Hradecka gained an early break advantage in the second set and levelled the match in a tight tenth game. The Czechs made the decisive move at 5-5 in the Match Tie-break, with two telling service returns helping to open up a 9-5 lead. They converted their second match point when Mladenovic netted a volley after 69 minutes. "I'm a little bit disappointed, but I thought they played pretty well at the end," said Nestor. "They played a good tie-breaker, and I'm a little bit upset that I lost serve first game in the second, because that kind of changed momentum, although we did have some chances to break back. I thought for the most part, even on the points where we had chances, they played pretty well. It wasn't us. It was more them playing pretty well."

Having finished runners-up in the Australian Open mixed doubles final in January (l. to Ebden/Gajdosova), Cermak and Hradecka clinched their first major title together.
